* Prior to its modern interpretation as a dry Martini with a pickled onion garnish, the Gibson was a singular cocktail at the turn of the nineteenth century. It was different from the Martini inasmuch as it was dry (both in amount and style of vermouth) and it contained no bitters, which were a mandatory if not a defining ingredient of a standard Martini. This made the Gibson the Martini that we all enjoy today.The addition of the onion came later.
